About

I'm a developer passionate about building accessible, user-friendly web applications that combine thoughtful design with solid engineering. My favorite work happens where design meets development, creating projects that not only look sharp but are also reliable, performant, and easy to use.

Currently, I'm a Web Developer Intern at the City of Los Angeles Department of Recreation & Parks, where I work on public-facing sites like SwimLA, PlayLA, and CLASS Parks, delivering Bootstrap 5 upgrades, accessibility improvements, and custom theming to keep them fast, inclusive, and easy to use.

Before this, I studied Computer Science at Cal State LA (graduating with Dean's List honors and a 3.8 GPA), and I've continued to grow as a developer through hands-on projects and coursework in React, Node.js, TypeScript, and full-stack JavaScript. Along the way, I've built and deployed a variety of apps, from portfolio-ready projects like unit converters and Chrome extensions to larger-scale applications like event management systems, AI-powered dashboards, and Supabase-integrated tools for hackathons.

In my free time, I'm usually building out side projects (like ChefQuest and InvoiceAi), contributing to hackathons, or leveling up my skills through online courses. Outside of code, I'm into sports (baseball, running, basketball), gaming, and hanging out with my chickens in the backyard.
